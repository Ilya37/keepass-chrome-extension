Privacy Policy

Last updated: February 7, 2026

Overview

KeePass Password Manager is a browser extension that stores and manages passwords locally on your device. It is designed with a zero-knowledge architecture: your data never leaves your browser, and the developer has no access to your passwords or master password.

Data Collection

This extension does not collect, transmit, or share any personal data. Specifically:

  • No analytics or telemetry of any kind.
  • No cookies or tracking pixels.
  • No network requests to external servers.
  • No crash reports sent anywhere.

Data Storage

All data is stored locally within your browser using Chrome Storage APIs:

  • chrome.storage.local — stores the encrypted .kdbx database file (AES-256 / ChaCha20 encryption, Argon2 key derivation). This data persists across browser restarts and is only removed when you uninstall the extension.
  • chrome.storage.session — stores the master password temporarily so the extension can re-open the database after a background service restart. This data is automatically cleared when you close the browser.

Encryption

Your password database is encrypted using the industry-standard KeePass .kdbx format with AES-256-CBC or ChaCha20 cipher and Argon2 key derivation. The master password is never stored in plaintext on disk — it exists only in volatile session storage while the browser is running.

Permissions

The extension requests the following browser permissions:

  • storage — to save the encrypted database locally.
  • alarms — auto-lock (15 min) and clipboard auto-clear.
  • activeTab — to fill passwords when you open the extension on a login page.
  • scripting — to fill fields into the current page when you click Fill.
  • clipboardWrite (optional) — to copy passwords; requested when you first copy.

Third-Party Services

This extension does not use any third-party services, APIs, or SDKs.

Children's Privacy

This extension is not directed at children under the age of 13.
